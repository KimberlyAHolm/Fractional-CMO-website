PBS Western Reserve

 

Strategic Leadership

 
 

At PBS Western Reserve, strategic marketing leadership involved creating mission-driven initiatives that deepened community connection and elevated the organization's voice. The following initiatives reflect work developed, shaped, and executed in close collaboration with a talented team.

 
   

Initiative

   

Community Impact Report

   
     

The station's first Community Impact Report was created to bring greater visibility to PBS Western Reserve's mission, community value, and regional impact. The report synthesizes the organization's work across education, programming, community outreach, and local storytelling into a clear, accessible narrative for donors, partners, government officials, and the public.

     

Developed through extensive cross-departmental collaboration, the report highlights measurable outcomes, community partnerships, and the station's role as a trusted resource. It also provides a framework for how the organization communicates its impact, helping staff, leadership, and the Board speak with greater clarity and consistency. The report has since become a cornerstone of the station's annual storytelling and engagement strategy.

   
   
         
 
 
   

Framework

   

Messaging Map

   
     

A station-wide messaging map was created to provide clear guidance on how to communicate in ways that consistently reflect the organization's mission. The framework now serves as a foundational tool for aligning internal and external communication and strengthening brand clarity across departments.

   
   
               
 
 
   

Advocacy Campaign

   

"Public Media Matters"

   
     

Public Media Matters was developed as a multi-platform initiative in response to renewed threats to federal and state funding for public media. The campaign provided clear, accessible information about potential funding cuts, answered frequently asked questions, corrected false claims circulating online, and equipped supporters with tools to contact their representatives.

   
   
         
   
     

Results

     
           
  • More than 16,700 viewers emailed their local representatives and senators
    •        
  • Thousands more called directly
    •        
  • Campaign drew interest from multiple local legislators who sought to better understand PBS Western Reserve's community impact
    •        
  • Reinforced the station's role as a trusted, transparent resource during a critical moment for public media
    •

Digital Strategy

   

Social Media Growth Through Content Strategy

   
     

Improving the effectiveness of social media began with building a stronger internal foundation and a strategy grounded in defined content pillars. Working collaboratively with the Social Media and Community Outreach Specialist, content pillars were established that aligned with strategic priorities: local and national programming promotion, station updates including events and community partnerships, and locally relevant user-generated content.

     

The impact was substantial. Facebook followers grew more than fivefold, reach expanded from thousands to over one million, and engagement increased across reactions, shares, and comments. Instagram and LinkedIn also saw steady growth. Monetization features were added on Meta, X, and YouTube, and a YouTube Shorts strategy was launched to drive viewers to long-form content, streaming, and broadcast.

   
   
     

Results

     
           
  • Facebook reach grew from thousands to over 1 million
    •        
  • Followers grew more than fivefold
    •        
  • Station moved from last to first among Ohio public media stations in social reach
    •        
  • Monetization features activated across Meta, X, and YouTube
    •

Content Marketing

 

Hiram College Awareness Campaigns

 
 

One of the first goals when joining Hiram College was to elevate awareness. The College had not had an active advertising campaign in more than a decade. The majority of high school students in Northeast Ohio were not familiar with the College or its location. Two back-to-back multifaceted campaigns helped make Hiram College appear like it was everywhere.

 
   
     

The campaigns relied heavily on digital advertising through social media and paid search on the Google Display Network. Campaign visuals and messaging were deliberately different from the typical college campaign — instead of focusing on facilities and programs, Hiram's story was told through its students, focusing on experiences and outcomes with bold, vibrant imagery.

     

Stories were introduced in a full-length recruitment video, edited into 15-second digital spots running on Google, YouTube, and TV in the Cleveland and Youngstown DMAs. Digital ads directed visitors to a landing page where they could learn more about each student before being invited to share contact information for follow-up nurturing by email, virtual tour, campus visit, or admission counselor conversation.
