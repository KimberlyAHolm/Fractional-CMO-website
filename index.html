About Kimberly

Meet Kimberly Holm

I did not start my career in marketing. I started in healthcare operations, which means before I ever wrote a creative brief or launched a campaign, I understood what it felt like to work inside an organization trying to accomplish important goals with limited resources. That perspective never left me. In many ways, it is still the most valuable thing I bring to my work today.

Over the past 30 years, I have worked in healthcare, higher education, public media, nonprofits, specialty insurance, and agency environments. I have been the client responsible for results, and I have been the partner responsible for delivering them. Those experiences taught me how to align strategy, leadership, and execution so organizations can move forward with confidence.

Throughout my career, I have led integrated marketing campaigns, developed brand and messaging strategies, mentored cross-functional teams, and built marketing systems designed to support long-term growth. Some of the work I am most proud of happened in organizations facing real constraints. Those situations often require the clearest thinking, the strongest leadership, and the most creative solutions.

One experience that continues to shape my work, especially with nonprofits, was overseeing individual giving and corporate support alongside marketing. That responsibility gave me a deeper understanding of donor psychology, stewardship, and the relationship between mission, messaging, and action. It also taught me that successful organizations communicate differently with every audience while remaining true to the same core purpose.

Clients and colleagues have often valued my ability to see both the strategic picture and the operational realities behind it. I believe good marketing leadership requires both. Strong ideas matter, but so does understanding how decisions affect teams, budgets, stakeholders, and the people doing the work every day.

I started this practice because I saw too many organizations doing meaningful work without access to senior marketing leadership. Fractional support is a practical way to close that gap. Organizations gain executive-level strategy, guidance, and accountability without the cost of a full-time hire.

Today I work with nonprofits, healthcare organizations, educational institutions, and growing businesses throughout Northeast Ohio as a Fractional CMO, marketing strategist, and trusted executive partner.

If that sounds like the kind of partnership you are looking for, I would welcome the opportunity to connect.

My Approach

How I work, and why it matters.

Serve the mission, move the numbers.

Strategy only works when it's tied to what your organization is trying to accomplish. I start with your mission and work outward from there, making sure every marketing decision has a reason behind it.

Build capacity, not dependency.

My goal is to leave your team stronger than I found it. I coach, mentor, and build systems that work long after I'm gone. The measure of good fractional leadership is what stays when the engagement ends.

Outcomes over deliverables.

A campaign that launches on time and misses the goal isn't a win. I measure success by results — awareness, pipeline, enrollment, loyalty, advocacy.

Integrity is non-negotiable.

I'll tell you what I actually think. If your strategy needs rethinking, I'll say so. You deserve a partner who's honest, not just agreeable.
